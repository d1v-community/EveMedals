## 3. Problem 区

### Section Label

Why This Matters

### Title

Meaningful player activity has no durable social form

### Body

In `EVE Frontier`, players explore, build, fight, transport, and support a living world. Those actions matter, but most of them remain trapped inside logs, isolated interfaces, or temporary moments that are hard to verify and even harder to share outside the game context.

### Problem Points

#### Point 1

Title: Invisible contribution  
Body: Real effort rarely becomes visible reputation.

#### Point 2

Title: Weak proof  
Body: Even legitimate contribution is difficult to prove outside the game context.

#### Point 3

Title: No portable identity layer  
Body: Players can share screenshots, but not structured, verifiable honor.

## 4. Solution 区

### Section Label

How It Works

### Title

From indexed activity to public honor

### Body

`EVE Medals` connects indexed player activity, achievement verification, on-chain medal ownership, and shareable Warrior identity in one product loop.

### Four-Step Flow

#### Step 1

Title: Index activity  
Body: Chronicle reads meaningful Frontier actions from the indexed activity window.

#### Step 2

Title: Verify achievement state  
Body: Player actions are mapped into medal progress, evidence, and readiness states.

#### Step 3

Title: Claim on-chain honor  
Body: Eligible medals can be claimed and reflected as wallet-bound honors on Sui.

#### Step 4

Title: Share public reputation  
Body: Warrior profiles and Medal cards turn achievement into visible, portable social assets.
